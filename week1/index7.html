Lisa Frank

Lisa Frank is one of the most iconic 1990's commercial artists. Her work was all over my school supplies as a child. I like her color schemes although some of it is very cluttered. Looking back now, I seem to be critiquing all of the art I see because that's what I do now. This is the new me. I'm a walking critic. Lisa, may your work live on to the present day even if it's too cluttered and needs more white space. Although, her color palette's are definitely what attracted me to her art. I like colorful art and designs. However, I'm currently working on making my own art with more balanced color. To have a distinctly bright palette is sometimes

shocking to the naked eye. Therefore, I feel that it must be in balance with the white space, composition, focus, and layout of the page. Although, sometimes I'm in a Lisa Frank kind of a mood where I like to see a brightly colored unicorn on my binder from 50 miles away. There is something about bright colors that you just can't run away from. They are loud, intense, and attention-grabbing. Not only do these nostalgic color palettes remind me of my childhood, but they exude this feeling of comfort. Usually she uses rainbow colors all in one piece. I like to do that in my own art as well. I'm currently trying to make it so my pieces have the right amount of white space. I have been known to pull a Lisa and try to fill the entire page with content and now I can see why that doesn't always work design-wise.
